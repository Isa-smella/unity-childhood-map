# Unity Childhood Map

A low-angle 3D exploration game prototype inspired by childhood maps, handmade worlds, hidden spaces, and small discoveries.

## Concept

This project is an early Unity prototype for a game about turning a childhood-style 2D imaginary map into a walkable 3D miniature world.

The player explores a handmade world from a low-angle perspective. Ordinary places may hide unexpected spaces: bushes may contain tunnels, bridges may have secret areas underneath, waterfalls may hide something behind them, and old ruins may suggest directions through murals or environmental clues.

The goal is not to build the largest open world, but to create a dense world that responds to curiosity.

## Current Prototype

The current prototype focuses on the first core interaction: discovering a distant place from a hill using a telescope.

Implemented features:

- Low-angle third-person player movement
- A small hill / ramp used as a lookout point
- A telescope interaction system
- UI prompts for using and exiting the telescope
- First-person-style telescope view
- Circular telescope mask
- Fog wall transparency while using the telescope
- Player movement locked while using the telescope
- A / D controls for rotating the telescope left and right
- Telescope rotation limited to ±30 degrees
- Telescope angle is preserved after exiting
- Telescope model and view point rotate together through a pivot system
- Git and GitHub project backup setup

## Planned Game Direction

The broader game idea includes:

- A main world where the player discovers a village through a hilltop telescope
- A village where the player is actually one of the villagers
- A player house with the player's name on it
- Village jobs for earning money
- A horse shop with normal purchasable horses and special mystery horses
- Mystery horses unlocked through later chapter tasks, hints, and maps
- A flower field with flower-picking tasks and a hidden peony flower easter egg
- Hidden spaces under bridges, inside bushes, behind waterfalls, and inside ruins
- A badge system that rewards the player for discovering secrets and completing unusual tasks
- Collectible souvenirs that can be displayed inside the player's house
- Indoor first-person exploration for examining details and collections

## Design Principles

- The world should reward curiosity.
- Small objects should feel meaningful.
- Ordinary places may hide second-layer spaces.
- Environmental hints should guide the player without direct instructions.
- Absurd ideas should still have gameplay value.
- The game should feel handmade rather than realistic.
- Outdoor gameplay mainly uses a low-angle third-person view.
- Indoor exploration mainly uses first-person view to show details clearly.
- The game should focus on interaction density rather than map size.

## Visual Direction

The intended style is a low-angle 3D handmade miniature world.

Possible visual references and elements:

- Paper-like objects
- Wooden supports
- Small handmade hills
- Fences and grass yards
- Stone paths and garden walls
- Bush tunnels made from branches
- Ruins with earth walls and spiral passages
- Small displayable collectibles
- A mix of pastoral calm and hidden strangeness

## Example Future Areas

### Village

A peaceful home base where the player can work, buy items, interact with villagers, and return after adventures.

### Player House

A large villa-like house with a fenced grass area, an attic, and space for displaying badges and collected souvenirs.

### Horse Shop

A shop that sells normal horses with money. Special “?” horses require clues, maps, and chapter-related tasks to unlock.

### Flower Field

A calm field for flower-related tasks. One hidden flower may connect to a real-world peony flower website as an easter egg.

### Waterfall Chapter

A boat is launched toward a waterfall, falls down, and becomes stranded near rocks. The boat and rocks form a visual clue pointing toward something hidden behind the waterfall.

### Ruins Chapter

An underground area inspired by earth-walled historical ruins, with tunnels, murals, and a mud-like spiral stair or ramp.

### Garden and Bridge Area

A small Chinese-style garden with water, stone paths, narrow alleys, stone walls, and a hidden space under a bridge.

### Bush Tunnel

A hidden passage inside a bush, with branches forming a natural tunnel-like entrance.

### Dinosaur Collectible

A miniature museum-style dinosaur skeleton model with a wooden base and metal supports. It can be collected, held, and displayed inside the player's house.

This is an early prototype. The current goal is to test core interactions and spatial feeling before building final art, full systems, or large maps.
